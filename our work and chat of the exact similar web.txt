**Yes, it's possible** but comes with significant trade-offs for your setup. Here's the reality:

**How it would work:**
- Modern browsers (Chrome 63+, Firefox) resolve `*.localhost` to `127.0.0.1` automatically — no `/etc/hosts` editing needed
- You'd add subdomain detection in both Vite (dev) and Express (prod) to route `login.localhost:3001` to the login page

**Problems you'd face:**

1. **Cookies won't share** between `localhost:3001` and `login.localhost:3001` — your auth flow would break since they're treated as different origins
2. **CORS issues** — API calls from `login.localhost:3001` to `localhost:3001/api/*` become cross-origin requests
3. **You have 6+ login pages** (`/login`, `/super-admin/login`, `/affiliate/login`, `/member/login`, etc.) — would each need its own subdomain?
4. **Safari and some browsers** don't support `*.localhost` subdomain resolution
5. **Development complexity** increases substantially for no functional gain

**My recommendation:** Path-based routing (`/login`) is the standard approach for SPAs and is what your entire stack (React Router, Vite, Express SPA fallback) is designed around. Subdomain routing is typically used when you have entirely separate apps (e.g., `app.example.com` vs `docs.example.com`), not for different pages within the same SPA.
